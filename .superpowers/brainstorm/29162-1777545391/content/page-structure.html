How much of the page do we keep?

Three approaches — same booking section, different amounts of supporting content

A

Ultra lean — booking only

Get to the calendar in one screen. Everything else removed. Best raw conversion rate but least context for cold visitors.

Short hero (headline + price)
▼ Booking section (free call + online + in-person)
▼ 2 testimonials (inline, no separate section)
▼ FAQ
B

Lean with credibility — recommended

Booking is right after the hero. A small block of credibility below it, then social proof. Cuts everything that doesn't directly earn the booking.

Short hero (headline + price + "Book Free Call" CTA)
▼ Booking section (free call hero + online + in-person)
▼ What you'll get (3 tight bullet points — no grid, no numbered labels)
▼ Pricing (inline — just the two numbers, no separate section)
▼ 2–3 testimonials
▼ FAQ
✂ Cut: The Case, The Guide, How It Works, Bulk Sessions (move to /booking or link from FAQ)
C

Keep most sections — just fix mobile + booking

Move booking to position 2 (right after hero), fix all mobile padding and grids, add sticky CTA bar on mobile. Keep the editorial content — The Case, The Guide, How It Works.

Hero (shorter on mobile)
▼ Booking section (moved up — now section 2)
▼ What You'll Learn
▼ Pricing
▼ The Case + The Guide
▼ How It Works
▼ Testimonials + FAQ
+ Sticky "Book Now" bar on mobile throughout

Why B is the recommendation

A — Ultra lean

✓ Fastest to booking

✓ Nothing to distract

✗ Cold visitors need some context before they trust you enough to book

B — Lean + credibility ★

✓ Booking in first two sections

✓ Enough context to convert cold visitors

✓ All fluff removed — every section earns its place

C — Fix + reorder

✓ Least work

✓ Keeps existing content

✗ Still too long — the editorial sections slow people down

This structure applies to both Tutoring and Studio pages. The Booking page (/booking) gets a similar treatment — short intro, then the three service cards immediately, no tall hero.
