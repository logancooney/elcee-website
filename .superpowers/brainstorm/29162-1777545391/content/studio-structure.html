Studio page — in depth

The studio offers two very different services: in-studio recording and remote mixing/mastering. That's the key design challenge.

The problem — two audiences, one page

Audience 1 — In-Studio Recording

Artists in Manchester who want to record. They care about the space, the engineer, and the rate. Book by the hour. Calendly booking.

£35/hr · 1–4hr sessions · Book calendar

Audience 2 — Remote Mix/Master

Artists anywhere who want their tracks mixed or mastered. They care about price, turnaround, and quality. Pay upfront, send files.

£40–£340 · Payment link · Send stems

How should the booking section handle both?

A

Two booking blocks — one per service type

Block 1 — Studio Recording
Free call (white, prominent) · Book Studio Time £35/hr → Calendly

Block 2 — Remote Mixing & Mastering
Full Mix+Master £340 · Vocal Mix £190 · Mastering £40 — each a direct payment link. No Calendly needed.

B

One booking block — free call leads, services beneath

Free 20-Min Call (white, prominent) → same Calendly
Book Studio Time £35/hr → Calendly
Mix/Master → links to a separate section lower on the page

Mixing prices live in their own section below, not in the booking block.

C

Two tabs — "Record" and "Mix/Master"

Toggle between two booking views. "Record" shows the Calendly booking options. "Mix/Master" shows the payment links with prices.

Keeps it in one compact block. Slightly more interactive.

Studio page structure (same B template as tutoring)

Proposed order:

① Hero — "The Alchemist Studio" + rate + free call CTA
② Booking section — (depends on A/B/C above)
③ What you get — 3 tight bullets (space, engineer, turnaround)
④ Testimonials
⑤ FAQ
✂ Cut: Services grid, Pricing tables, Facility section, How It Works, separate Remote Services section

Pricing info folds into the booking options themselves — you see the price when you see the CTA. No separate pricing section needed.

One more question — the studio rate

The current page shows £35/hr standard and £30/hr loyalty (£240/month subscription). Should the loyalty plan be on this page, or is that a conversation you have after the free call?

A

Hide loyalty plan — just show £35/hr

Simpler. Loyalty is something you offer after the relationship is built. Don't complicate the booking page with it.

B

Show loyalty plan — it's a selling point

Artists who record regularly will be attracted by the discount. Small mention alongside the standard rate.
